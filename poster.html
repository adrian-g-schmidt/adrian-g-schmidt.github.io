Australian Cybernetic Poster

Australian Cybernetic Poster

Role: Interaction development

Description: I developed the interactive component of the Australian Cybernetic: A Point Through Time artwork designed by KopiSu. They created a set of animations to be displayed on a vertical screen, and proposed that it could be dynamic based on the distance of the viewer. I did this by using PoseNet - a model that assigns a pixels position to different parts of a body on camera. Although I experimented with models that make a three dimensional estimate, I had better results using the two dimensional data and measuring the pixel count between the two shoulders. After installing, I adjusted the variables to suit the environment, and different userâ€™s shoulder widths. When the viewer gets close enough to fully fill the page with pink, a new animation plays. When a person is not detected by the camera, a passive animation plays that can quickly transition over to the dynamic state.
